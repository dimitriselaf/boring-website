Stefanos Kasselakis' Use of TikTok in SYRIZA Elections

How an outsider used social media to lead Greece's main opposition party

By Dimitris Elafropoulos

Stefanos Kasselakis’ rise to the leadership of Syriza, Greece's main opposition party, is as remarkable as it is unprecedented, and much of his success can be attributed to his savvy use of TikTok. In a matter of weeks, the Greek-American entrepreneur captivated a significant portion of the electorate with his political videos on the platform, showcasing his outsider status and fresh perspective.

Kasselakis, who had no prior political experience in Greece, announced his candidacy only four weeks before the election. His TikTok campaign allowed him to bypass traditional media channels and directly engage with a younger, digitally native audience. Through short, engaging clips, he communicated his vision for a reformed Syriza and a modernized Greece, tapping into the public’s desire for change and innovation.

His strategy proved effective. Kasselakis won 56.69% of the vote, defeating Efi Achtsioglou, a former labour minister and the perceived frontrunner. His use of TikTok not only democratized his campaign by making it more accessible but also underscored his message of bringing Syriza closer to contemporary political movements akin to the US Democratic Party.

Kasselakis’ victory has ushered in a new era for Syriza, marking a departure from its traditional leftist roots towards a broader, more centrist appeal. By leveraging TikTok, he demonstrated how social media can be a powerful tool for political engagement and transformation, reshaping the landscape of Greek politics in a way that few could have anticipated.
